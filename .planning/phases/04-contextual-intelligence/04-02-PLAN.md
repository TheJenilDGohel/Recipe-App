<objective>
Implement the core "Intelligence" of the phase: determining the user's current context (Time of Day and Location-based Area) and fetching relevant recipes.

Purpose: Translate raw time and location data into meaningful recipe discovery queries.
Output: `LocationService` for fetching coordinates/address and `ContextualDiscoveryProvider` for recipe suggestions.
</objective>

<feature>
  <name>Contextual Discovery Engine</name>
  <files>lib/src/features/recipes/presentation/providers/contextual_discovery_provider.dart, test/features/recipes/presentation/providers/contextual_discovery_provider_test.dart</files>
  <behavior>
    - If time is between 6 AM and 10 AM, suggest "Breakfast" category.
    - If time is between 11 AM and 2 PM, suggest "Lunch" (mapped to 'Side' or 'Main' or specific lunch-friendly categories if available, else 'Seafood/Chicken'). UI Spec says "Lunchtime Favorites". Let's use 'Pasta' or 'Seafood' or similar if 'Lunch' isn't a direct category. Actually, let's use 'Breakfast', 'Pasta', 'Seafood' as proxies.
    - If time is between 5 PM and 9 PM, suggest "Dinner" (proxy: 'Beef' or 'Pork' or 'Chicken').
    - If location is available and maps to a known TheMealDB Area (e.g., India -> Indian), prioritize that area.
    - If location is unavailable or unknown, fallback to "Global" popular recipes (use `getRandomMeal` or a fixed popular category like 'Dessert').
  </behavior>
  <implementation>
    - Use `geolocator` to get `Position` in `LocationService`.
    - Use `geocoding` to convert `Position` to `Placemark` and extract `country`.
    - Map `country` to `TheMealDB Area` using a internal Map.
    - `ContextualDiscoveryProvider` (FutureProvider) combines time and location to call `filterByCategory` or `filterByArea`.
  </implementation>
</feature>
